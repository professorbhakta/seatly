# Origin and motivation

## The everyday problem

People using GSRTC-style state transport buses often cannot:

- see upcoming buses for their stand or a from→to route
- understand timing and line-up clearly
- know seat availability — or when a bus is full with standing passengers
- track a bus live without confusion or juggling multiple apps

Passengers arrive at stands uncertain. Conductors work with Electronic Ticket Machine (ETM) interfaces that feel boring, hard to use, and disconnected from what passengers see.

## Founder intent

The goal is **awareness and an easier process** for everyone — passenger and conductor.

This is not “life-changing logistics” hype. It is:

- clearer information before boarding
- a modern, seat-map-first conductor experience
- occupancy that comes from real ticket issuance, not manual guesswork

## What triggered Seatly

1. **Passenger gap** — RedBus-style apps focus on booking; stand-level line-up and standing-passenger visibility are weak for GSRTC-style travel.
2. **Conductor gap** — Current ETM UIs feel dated. Selling a seat should start from the seat map, not from separate “mark filled” steps.
3. **GSRTC-first spiral** — Start with Gujarat State Road Transport Corporation patterns (routes, stands, conductors), prove the demo, expand later only if it works.

## Scope honesty (v0.1)

Seatly v0.1 is a **demo**: sample Gujarat stations, simulated UPI, no official GSRTC affiliation. The motivation is real; production data and partnerships come in later spirals.

## Tagline direction

**Asaan sawari — know before you board.**

Simple Indian voice: easy journey, informed before you get on the bus.
